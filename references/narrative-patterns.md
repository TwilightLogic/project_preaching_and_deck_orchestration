# Narrative and Evidence Patterns

Use this file when choosing the audience lens, narrative spine, and wording strength.

## Audience Lenses

- Technical audience: emphasize mechanism, system design, why the approach works, and trade-offs.
- Product or business audience: emphasize user pain, workflow improvement, adoption, and leverage.
- Leadership or decision-maker audience: emphasize why now, strategic fit, risk reduction, and team leverage.
- Investor or partner audience: emphasize market timing, differentiated position, proof, and expansion potential.
- Interview or review audience: emphasize judgment, execution quality, and what was learned.

State the inferred audience when it is not explicit.

## Narrative Options

- Problem -> Solution -> Evidence -> Vision
- Market or Trend -> Gap -> Our Approach -> Proof
- User Pain Point -> Product Journey -> Impact
- Technical Insight -> System Design -> Why It Matters
- Before / After comparison

Compare at least three options before recommending one.

## Evidence Strength

- Direct evidence: measured results, experiments, user quotes, shipped outcomes, or verified comparisons.
- Indirect evidence: architectural advantages, informed inference, qualitative observations, or adjacent results.
- Assumption: a plausible but unverified belief that still needs proof.
- Missing information: evidence the story would benefit from but does not yet have.

Do not blur these categories together.

## Wording Guidance

Prefer:
- "suggests" instead of "proves" when evidence is indirect
- "likely advantage" instead of "clear advantage" when the comparison is incomplete
- "early signal" instead of "validated outcome" when scale is limited
- "can reduce" instead of "reduces" when no measured delta exists

Avoid unsupported phrases such as:
- "industry-leading"
- "best"
- "significant improvement"
- "high ROI"
- "revolutionary"

If a point is still an assumption, say what proof would strengthen it.
